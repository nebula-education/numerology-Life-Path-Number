Section 01 · Foundation

A direction, not a destiny

The Life Path Number shows the general direction of your life — not one event or one choice, but the whole path. The recurring themes, the natural strengths, the lessons that keep showing up.

A lot of people don't understand why the same patterns keep appearing in their life. Why one person always ends up in situations requiring strength and responsibility. Why another keeps learning through relationships. Why a third can't live without freedom and change.

The Life Path Number doesn't give a verdict. It doesn't say: you can only be this way and no other. But it does highlight the main theme that life tends to return to — a language that makes invisible patterns visible.

1
Recognize your strengths The natural capacities that flow easily — the ones you often take for granted because they've always been there.
2

3

The Digital
Root

Section 02 · How it works

Numbers 1 through 9

The foundation of numerology is the numbers 1 to 9. Every more complex number is always reduced to one of these — or to a master number. Think of each as a different theme, a different tone of life.

The main operation in numerology is called the digital root — we add all the digits of a number together. If we get a two-digit result, we add again. We keep going until we reach a single number from 1 to 9.

No complex formulas. Just careful addition. And one important exception: master numbers.

Example A

1 + 2 + 3 = 6

Already a single digit. Done.

Example B

1 + 2 + 3 + 4 = 10 → 1

Two digits: add again. 1 + 0 = 1.

How to do it

Three steps

1
Write out all digits separately Day, month, and year of your birth date — all digits in sequence.
2
Add them all together Sum every digit. If you get a two-digit result, add those digits again.
3
Stop at 1–9, 11, or 22 Keep reducing until you reach a single digit — unless you hit a master number, in which case you stop.

Section 03 · The exception

11 & 22 — amplified potential

In numerology, certain two-digit numbers are considered special. If your calculation lands on one of these, you stop. You don't reduce further.

A master number doesn't mean a person is automatically special or "better." It means amplified potential — more energy, more tension, more inner intensity. Like a stronger charge: it can produce bigger results, but it requires more maturity to handle well.

Master numbers are less of a gift and more of a potential a person still needs to learn how to live with.

Master Number

11 — Intuition & Sensitivity

Deep feeling, strong intuition, the ability to inspire. Can be overwhelmed by noise and others' emotions. The task: hold the sensitivity as a strength, not a burden.

Master Number

22 — Vision & Building

Big-picture thinking, the ability to turn grand visions into real structures. Can fear their own scale. The task: translate potential into concrete, step-by-step action.

When to stop — example
  • ·2 + 0 + 1 + 8 = 11 — stop here. Master number.
  • ·1 + 9 + 8 + 4 = 22 — stop here. Master number.
  • ·1 + 9 + 9 + 0 = 19 → 1 + 9 = 10 → 1 + 0 = 1 — keep reducing.

Your full date of birth

Add all the digits of your date of birth — day, month, and year — then reduce using the digital root. Stop at 1–9, 11, or 22.
